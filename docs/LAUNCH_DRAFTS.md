## KVR — product listing description

**Title:** ÉTER PRISMA — spectral dispersion with an exact group-delay curve

**Short description (1 línea):**
PRISMA smears sound across time the way a prism splits light: every frequency arrives with its own delay, following a group-delay curve you shape directly.

**Full description:**

PRISMA is a spectral dispersion effect. Not an allpass chain approximating the idea: the group-delay curve D(f) you set is rendered as an exact FIR kernel and convolved in real time, verified against the mathematical reference below −140 dB.

Turn Spread and a drum hit becomes a rainbow riser where the highs arrive late. Flip Tilt and it becomes an alien drop where the lows drag behind. Shape bends the curve from liquid sweeps to laser chirps.

Features:
- Spread 0–2 s, Tilt fully morphable between arco and caída, Shape 0.3–2.5, band limits F Min/F Max
- Click-free automation: kernels recompute on a worker thread and crossfade in, zero allocations on the audio thread
- Optical-bench GUI: a living prism whose fan IS your delay curve, photons traveling with their real per-frequency delay, particle spectrometer, phosphor oscilloscope
- VST3 + CLAP, Windows x64, GPL-3.0, free and open source
- pluginval strictness 10 clean

First device of the ÉTER line.

**Developer:** Juan Cruz Maisú
**License:** Freeware / open source (GPL-3.0)
**Links:** https://github.com/Jc-asastu/eter-prisma
